PISA 2012 - The relationship of student attitudes to results

8202 students in 507 schools accross the uk took part in the 2012 pisa Survey. The students answered a questionaire investigating, among other things, their attitude towards school and were graded on their abilities in maths, science and reading. An additional questionaire was filled out by the senior administrator in each school.

The chart shows how students who showed positive attitudes towards school in the PISA questionaire tended to have higher scores in the PISA tests for maths, science and reading. The relationship remained the same accross different groups of schools.

The responses to different attitude statements can be explored by changing the 'Students Attitudes' drop down box. The schools included in the charts can by filtered, either by the schools average score or by the responses of the schools senior administrator to questions in the school questionaire. The scoring subject(s) can be changed and a second chart can be added to the visualisation to make comparisons easier.
